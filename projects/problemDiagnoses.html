Balancing Innovation and Prestige: A Strategic Framework to Restore DriveFun's Market Edge Without Compromising Brand Integrity.

project brief

Role - Business Analyst

Key deliverables - Strategic Analysis, Market research, Financial Modelling, Brand Management, Innovative Management

Executive Summary

DriveFun, a high-end sports car manufacturer, faced declining profitability due to increased market saturation and competition. Tasked by DriveFun’s CEO, I led a strategic analysis and developed a framework to restore profitability while protecting the company’s luxury brand status. The case study below outlines the approach, analysis, and recommendations to achieve sustainable growth without compromising DriveFun’s premium image.

Category Description
Achieved Results Established high-end brand reputation in the sports car segment, past profitability in the 1990s.
Disturbing Events Profitability decline since early 2000s due to market saturation and increased competition.
Desired Result Restore and sustain profitability without diluting high-end brand reputation.
Key Questions
  • What strategic options exist to retain high-end status while improving profits?
  • How can DriveFun differentiate to compete more effectively in a saturated market?
  • What innovations (e.g., technology, sustainable features) could attract high-end customers?
Stakeholders
  • CEO and executive team
  • Existing high-end customers
  • Sales and marketing team
  • Suppliers and manufacturing partners
Constraints
  • Cannot release lower-priced models due to brand positioning concerns.
  • Limited resources, with current profitability constraints.
  • High production costs associated with high-end sports cars
Decision Criteria
  • Ability to increase profitability and customer base without brand dilution.
  • Customer appeal and alignment with high-end brand perception.
  • Market competitiveness and long-term sustainability of chosen strategy.
